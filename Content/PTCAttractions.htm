Peachtree City Attractions

There are many places to visit in Peachtree City. Three of these are Lake Peachtree, the Fredrick Brown Jr. Amphitheater, and Flat Creek Nature Area.

Lake Peachtree

Lake Peachtree is located in the heart of Peachtree City. With it being in the heart, it is a main attraction for both residents and visitors. It is a terrific place to take a walk or cruise around on a golf cart. With its amazing foliage, Lake Peachtree is the place to be for a relaxing day around nature.

 

The Fredrick Brown Jr. Amphitheater

If you have interest in the Arts, the Fred is the place to be! The Fredrick Brown Jr. Amphitheater has been hosting events since 1976. In fact, the very first play ever shown in the Fred was "The McIntosh Trail". Currently the amphitheater is used as a concert venue for national and international touring acts.

This 1,6000-seat outdoor facility is guaranteed to be a good time! Make sure to look out for future concert dates at the Fredrick Brown Jr. Amphitheater!

 

Flat Creek Nature Area

Flat Creek Nature Area is a 513-acre wetland in Peachtree City that extends 3.5 miles along Flat Creek. Flat Creek also has a 1200-foot boardwalk where you can walk with nature! There are also viewing platforms and benches for those who wish to slow down and take in the Area's beauty. Many people ask, "Is Flat Creek Nature Area Golf Cart accessible?", and the answer is yes! Not all of the area is accessible by golf cart, but you can find a parking spot and begin your adventure by foot if you want the entire experience.
